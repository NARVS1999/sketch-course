Pencil Grades & Their Uses

GradeHardnessBest For
2HHardLight construction lines, guidelines, faint sketch
HBMediumGeneral sketching, writing, mid-tones
2BSoftShading, darker lines, good all-rounder
4BSofterDeep shadows, broad shading
6BVery softDark darks, large shadow areas, expressive marks
8BSoftestBlackest blacks, final dark accents

Rule of thumb: Start light (2H/HB) for construction, build up to soft (2B–6B) for shadows. Hard pencils first — they erase cleanly.

Perspective Rules

1-Point Perspective

  • One vanishing point (VP) on the horizon line (HL)
  • All depth lines converge to that VP
  • Vertical and horizontal lines stay parallel to the page edges
  • Best for: hallways, roads, front-facing rooms

2-Point Perspective

  • Two vanishing points on the horizon line (left and right)
  • All horizontal lines converge to one of the two VPs
  • Vertical lines stay vertical
  • Best for: buildings from a corner, cubes, exterior scenes

Facial Proportions (Loomis Method)

  • Head ≈ 5 eye-widths wide
  • Eyes sit halfway between top of head and chin
  • Bottom of nose is halfway between eyes and chin
  • Mouth sits halfway between nose and chin
  • Ears align between brow line and nose line
  • Corners of mouth align with center of pupils

Figure Proportions

  • Adult body ≈ 7.5–8 heads tall
  • Shoulders ≈ 2–3 heads wide
  • Hands ≈ face-sized
  • Elbow aligns with waist
  • Wrist aligns with crotch
  • Knees roughly halfway down the leg

Composition: Rule of Thirds

Divide your page into a 3×3 grid. Place key elements (focal point, horizon) along the grid lines or at their intersections. This creates a more dynamic composition than centering everything.
