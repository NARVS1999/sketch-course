Sketching Glossary

Key terms used throughout this course — the language of sketching.

Contour

The outline or edge defining a form. Contour drawing trains you to follow edges with your eye and hand simultaneously.

Avoid: Outlining, tracing

Hatching

A shading technique using parallel lines. Closer lines = darker value; wider spacing = lighter value.

Avoid: Scribble shading, messy lines

Cross-hatching

Layers of hatching at different angles (usually perpendicular) to build deeper values and texture.

Avoid: Grid shading, random marks

Blending

Smoothing graphite with a tool (stump, tissue, finger) to create a seamless gradient. Best for soft transitions like skin or sky.

Avoid: Over-blending (muddy look)

Value

How light or dark a tone is. A value scale ranges from white (highlight) through mid-tones to black (shadow).

Avoid: Brightness, color intensity

Highlight

The brightest spot on a form where light hits directly. Usually left as white paper.

Core Shadow

The darkest part of the shadow on an object, just beyond the terminator (where light stops hitting directly).

Cast Shadow

The shadow an object throws onto another surface. Its shape tells you about the light source and the receiving surface.

Vanishing Point (VP)

The point on the horizon line where parallel lines appear to converge in perspective drawing.

Horizon Line (HL)

The line representing the viewer's eye level in a perspective drawing. All vanishing points sit on this line.

Foreshortening

The visual compression of an object or body part extending toward or away from the viewer, creating the illusion of depth.

Loomis Method

A constructive approach to drawing the head using a sphere, jaw plane, and guidelines to place features proportionally. Developed by Andrew Loomis.

Gesture

A quick, loose sketch capturing the movement and energy of a pose — not the details. Usually done in 30 seconds to 2 minutes.

Avoid: Stiff poses, anatomical details in gesture

Negative Space

The shapes and spaces around and between the subject. Drawing negative space helps you see edges more accurately.

Sight-Size

A measuring technique where you hold your pencil at arm's length, align it with a part of your subject, and transfer that measurement to your paper.

Tip Refer back to this glossary whenever you encounter an unfamiliar term in a lesson. These terms are used consistently throughout the course.
