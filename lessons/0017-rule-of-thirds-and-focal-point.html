Lesson 0017: Rule of Thirds & Focal Point

Plan your drawing with a 3x3 grid and direct the viewer's eye.

Phase 5: Advanced Composition & Style

Lesson Goal Plan a composition using the rule of thirds grid, placing the focal point at an intersection for maximum visual impact.

What Is Composition?

Composition is how you arrange the elements in your drawing. Good composition guides the viewer's eye around the page and makes the drawing feel balanced and intentional.

The Rule of Thirds

Divide your page into a 3x3 grid (two horizontal lines, two vertical lines). The key principle: place your most important element (the focal point) at one of the four intersections, NOT in the center.

1

Lightly draw a 3x3 grid on your page (or imagine it).

2

Decide on your focal point — the main subject (a tree, a face, a building).

3

Place the focal point at one of the four grid intersections, not dead center.

4

Align other elements (horizon, edges, secondary objects) along the grid lines.

Guiding the Eye

Use lines in your composition to lead the viewer's eye toward the focal point:

  • Leading lines — roads, rivers, fences that point toward the subject
  • Contrast — the brightest and darkest areas naturally attract attention
  • Detail — the most detailed area pulls focus; simplify areas farther from the focal point
Tip Don't just center your subject. Off-center placement feels more dynamic and professional. Next time you look at a movie poster or photograph, notice how often the subject is at a rule-of-thirds intersection.

Mini-Project: 3 Thumbnail Compositions

Draw 3 small thumbnail sketches (about 5×7 cm each) of the same scene — a tree on a hill, for example. In the first, center the tree. In the second, place it on the left third intersection. In the third, place it on the bottom-right intersection with a road leading to it. Compare which feels most dynamic.

Summary

  • Rule of thirds: 3x3 grid, place focal point at intersections
  • Off-center compositions feel more dynamic than centered ones
  • Use leading lines, contrast, and detail to guide the eye
  • Thumbnail sketches are a fast way to test compositions
