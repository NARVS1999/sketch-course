Lesson 0014: Features Study — Eyes & Nose

Draw realistic eyes with iris reflection and nose with proper shadow placement.

Phase 4: Portrait & Anatomy

Lesson Goal Draw a realistic eye (with iris and catchlight) and a nose (with underside shading).

The Eye

The eye is not a flat almond shape. It sits inside a socket, wrapped by lids, and has volume. Understanding its structure is the key to drawing it from any angle.

Eye Structure

  • Eyeball — a sphere sitting in the socket
  • Upper lid — curves over the ball, casts a shadow on the iris
  • Lower lid — thinner, less prominent
  • Iris — circular, partially hidden by the upper lid
  • Catchlight — the reflection of the light source on the eye (makes it look alive)

Drawing the Eye

1

Draw the almond shape — the opening between the lids.

2

Draw the full circle of the iris (extending under the upper lid). Add the pupil in the center.

3

Add the catchlight — a small white spot near the top of the iris. Darken the iris around the pupil.

4

Add the upper lid's cast shadow on the eyeball. Draw eyelashes growing outward from the lid edge.

The Nose

The nose is a wedge shape with three main parts: the bridge (top), the ball (tip), and the nostrils. The most important rule: shade under it.

1

Draw the nose as a simple wedge shape (triangle in front view, L-shape in profile).

2

Draw the nostrils as curved teardrops. The bridge has subtle side shading, not a hard outline.

3

The most important part: add a dark shadow under the nose where it meets the upper lip. Add soft shading on the sides of the bridge.

Tip The biggest beginner mistake: outlining the nose with a hard line. In reality, the nose is defined by shadows — shade the sides and underside, but let the bridge be defined by the highlight.

Mini-Project: Eye & Nose Study Page

Fill one page with eye studies and one page with nose studies. Include: front view, 3/4 view, and profile for each. Use a reference photo or mirror. Focus on shading, not outlines.

Summary

  • The eye is a sphere with lids wrapping over it
  • The catchlight makes the eye look alive — always include it
  • The nose is a wedge defined by shadows, not outlines
  • Shadow under the nose and on the sides of the bridge
