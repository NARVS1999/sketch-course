Lesson 0015: Features Study — Lips & Ears

Master lip mapping (upper and lower lip) and the complex curves of the ear.

Phase 4: Portrait & Anatomy

Lesson Goal Draw realistic lips (front view) and ears from multiple angles using structural guides.

The Lips

The lips are a curved, three-dimensional form wrapping around the teeth and jaw. The upper lip has a distinct M-shape (the Cupid's bow), and the lower lip is fuller with a subtle shadow underneath.

Drawing Lips — Front View

1

Draw a horizontal line — the mouth line where the lips meet.

2

Draw the upper lip: start from the center (Cupid's bow) and curve down to the corners. The upper lip has two distinct peaks.

3

Draw the lower lip: a fuller, C-curve shape underneath. Add the philtrum (the vertical groove from nose to upper lip).

4

Shade: the upper lip is usually darker (it faces downward, away from light). The lower lip is lighter with a highlight. Add shadow under the lower lip on the chin.

The Ear

The ear is a complex shape, but it follows a consistent structure. Once you learn the basic anatomy, it becomes much easier.

Ear Anatomy

  • Helix — the outer ridge that curves around the ear
  • Anti-helix — the inner ridge parallel to the helix
  • Concha — the deep bowl in the center
  • Lobe — the soft bottom part
  • Tragus — the small bump in front of the ear canal
1

Draw an oval shape — the general outline of the ear.

2

Inside, draw the Y-shape of the anti-helix. Add the helix as the outer rim.

3

Darken the concha (the deep bowl). Add the lobe and tragus.

Tip Ears are often overlooked, but getting them right makes a portrait much more convincing. Remember: the top of the ear aligns with the brow line, and the bottom aligns with the nose line.

Quick Quiz

1. Which lip is usually shaded darker?
2. Where do the top and bottom of the ear align on the face?

Summary

  • Upper lip has Cupid's bow; lower lip is fuller with a highlight
  • Upper lip is usually darker (faces downward)
  • Ear aligns between brow line and nose line
  • Key ear anatomy: helix, anti-helix, concha, lobe, tragus
