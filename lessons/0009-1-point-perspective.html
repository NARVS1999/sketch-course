Lesson 0009: 1-Point Perspective

Draw a road or hallway shrinking toward a single vanishing point.

Phase 3: Perspective & Proportion

Lesson Goal Draw a scene with 1-point perspective: a road with telephone poles or a hallway with doors.

What Is 1-Point Perspective?

It's a way of drawing that makes flat things look 3D. All parallel lines that recede into the distance converge at a single vanishing point (VP) on the horizon line (HL). Everything else stays vertical or horizontal.

Key Terms

  • Horizon Line (HL) — your eye level. Everything above it is seen from below; everything below it is seen from above.
  • Vanishing Point (VP) — where all receding lines meet. Usually centered.
  • Orthogonal lines — the diagonal lines that converge at the VP (road edges, building tops).

Draw a Road

1

Draw a horizontal line across your page — this is your horizon line.

2

Place a dot in the center of the horizon line — your vanishing point.

3

From the bottom edge of your page, draw two diagonal lines converging at the VP. This is your road.

4

Add telephone poles: draw vertical lines on each side of the road. Make them smaller and closer together as they approach the VP.

5

Add a center line (dashed) converging at the VP. Add a sky and some clouds above the HL.

Draw a Hallway

Same concept, but indoors. The VP is at the end of the hallway. Doors, windows, and floor tiles all shrink toward the VP.

Tip All vertical lines stay perfectly vertical. All horizontal lines stay perfectly horizontal. Only the depth lines (orthogonals) angle toward the VP. If you remember this, 1-point perspective is foolproof.

Quick Quiz

1. Where do all receding lines meet in 1-point perspective?
2. What do vertical and horizontal lines do in 1-point perspective?

Mini-Project: 1-Point Room

Draw a room from a 1-point perspective: a living room, bedroom, or classroom. Include at least 3 objects (table, chair, window) drawn in correct perspective. The back wall should be a rectangle with the VP at its center.

Summary

  • 1 VP on the HL — all depth lines converge there
  • Vertical and horizontal lines stay parallel to page edges
  • Objects get smaller and closer together as they approach the VP
  • Great for roads, hallways, rooms, and interior scenes
