Lesson 0013: Facial Proportions

Using the Loomis method to construct a realistic head and place the features.

Phase 4: Portrait & Anatomy

Lesson Goal Draw a front-facing head using the Loomis method — a sphere + jaw construction with accurate feature guidelines.

The Loomis Method

The Loomis method, developed by Andrew Loomis, is a systematic way to draw the head. You start with a sphere, add a jaw, and then place guidelines for the features. It works because it's based on real head proportions, not guesswork.

Front View Construction

1

Draw a circle. This is the cranium (the top part of the skull).

2

Draw a vertical line down the center and a horizontal line at the halfway point. The eyes go on this line.

3

Add the jaw: a V-shape from the sides of the circle down to the chin. The chin is one face-width below the bottom of the nose.

4

Add guidelines: nose line (halfway between eyes and chin), mouth line (halfway between nose and chin). Ears go between the eye line and nose line.

The Golden Ratios of the Face

  • Eyes sit halfway between top of head and chin
  • Bottom of nose is halfway between eyes and chin
  • Mouth is halfway between nose and chin
  • The face is about 5 eye-widths wide
  • One eye-width space between the eyes
  • Corners of the mouth align with the center of the pupils
Common Mistake Placing the eyes too high on the head. Remember: the eyes are at the halfway point between the top of the skull and the chin. Beginners instinctively put them higher because they think of "head" as just the face.

Mini-Project: Loomis Head Practice

Draw 5 front-view heads using the Loomis construction method. The first 3 should follow the proportions exactly (textbook face). For the last 2, try varying the proportions slightly (longer nose, wider eyes) to see how it changes the look.

Summary

  • Loomis method = circle (cranium) + jaw + guidelines
  • Eyes at the halfway point of the head
  • Face is 5 eye-widths wide
  • Nose halfway between eyes and chin; mouth halfway between nose and chin
