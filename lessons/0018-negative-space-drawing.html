Lesson 0018: Negative Space Drawing

Instead of drawing the object, draw the space around it.

Phase 5: Advanced Composition & Style

Lesson Goal Draw an object by drawing only the negative space around it — training spatial awareness and edge perception.

What Is Negative Space?

Positive space = the object itself. Negative space = the shapes and gaps around and between objects. When you draw negative space, you're forced to see edges instead of symbols. It's a powerful perception hack.

The Chair Exercise

This is the classic negative space exercise, popularized by Betty Edwards.

1

Place a chair in front of you (or use a photo of one).

2

Instead of drawing the chair's legs and seat, draw the shapes of the gaps between them — the space under the seat, between the legs, between the back slats.

3

Shade these negative spaces dark, leaving the chair itself as white paper.

4

Step back. You should see a perfectly recognizable chair, even though you never drew a single "chair part."

Why This Works

Your brain has strong "symbol" habits — it knows what a chair looks like and wants to draw a simplified symbol instead of the real thing. Negative space short-circuits this habit. When you focus on the spaces, your brain has no symbol for "gap under chair" — so it draws what's actually there.

Tip The most common use of negative space in daily drawing: checking proportions. The gap between two objects (or between parts of an object) is just as important as the objects themselves. Train yourself to see both.

Mini-Project: Negative Space Still Life

Set up a still life with 2–3 objects (a cup, scissors, and a plant, for example). Draw only the negative spaces between them. Shade the spaces dark and leave the objects as white paper. The objects should emerge from the dark areas.

Summary

  • Negative space = the shapes around and between objects
  • Drawing negative space forces you to see real edges, not symbols
  • The chair exercise is the classic negative space drill
  • Use negative space awareness to check proportions in all your drawings
