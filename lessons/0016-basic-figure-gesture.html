Lesson 0016: Basic Figure Gesture

Quick 30-second sketches to capture the movement and energy of a pose.

Phase 4: Portrait & Anatomy

Lesson Goal Draw quick gesture sketches (30 seconds) that capture the action and flow of a pose — not the details.

What Is Gesture Drawing?

Gesture drawing is NOT about drawing the figure accurately. It's about capturing the movement, energy, and flow of a pose in as few lines as possible. It's the opposite of careful, detailed drawing — and it's essential for making your figures feel alive.

The Method

1

Set a timer for 30 seconds. Use a reference photo or a real person.

2

Start with a single flowing line — the "line of action" — that captures the main curve of the pose (the spine's curve).

3

Add a circle for the head, a ribcage shape, and a pelvis shape. Connect them with lines for the spine.

4

Add single lines for arms and legs showing direction and energy. Don't draw hands or feet — just the flow.

Tips for Great Gestures

  • Draw from your shoulder — big, sweeping motions
  • Exaggerate the curve — make the action clearer than it actually is
  • Don't stop — keep the pencil moving even when you're unsure
  • Quantity over quality — do 20 quick sketches, not 1 detailed one

Mini-Project: 30-Second Gesture Marathon

Go to Line of Action and do 20 gesture drawings at 30 seconds each. Don't judge them. The goal is to loosen up and learn to see the big shapes and flow. After 20, do 5 more at 2 minutes each for slightly more detail.

Summary

  • Gesture = movement and energy, not details
  • Start with the line of action — the main curve of the pose
  • 30 seconds per sketch — keep moving
  • Exaggerate the curve to make poses read clearly
