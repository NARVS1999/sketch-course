Lesson 0019: Experimental Mark-Making

Explore different mediums and expressive strokes to find your voice.

Phase 5: Advanced Composition & Style

Lesson Goal Experiment with at least two different mediums (charcoal, ink, or watercolor pencil) and three mark-making techniques (stippling, scumbling, expressive line work).

Finding Your Style

Up to now, you've been learning fundamentals — the universal skills every artist needs. Now it's time to explore. Your personal style emerges when you experiment with different tools and techniques and discover what feels natural and exciting to you.

Three Techniques to Try

Stippling

Building value with dots. More dots = darker. Fewer dots = lighter. It's slow but produces beautiful, textured results. Try it with a fine pen or sharp pencil.

Scumbling

Random, circular scribbles that layer to create soft, textured shading. Great for charcoal or soft pencil. It gives a loose, energetic feel.

Expressive Line Work

Varying line thickness, speed, and pressure within a single stroke. Thick-to-thin lines, jagged lines, flowing lines — let your hand be free and expressive.

Mediums to Explore

  • Charcoal — soft, blendable, dramatic. Best for quick, bold sketches
  • Ink pen — permanent, forces commitment. Best for crisp, confident line work
  • Watercolor pencil — draw then brush water for painterly effects. Best for hybrid drawings
  • Your usual graphite — but try holding it differently (underhand grip for loose shading)

Tip Don't worry about "mistakes" with new mediums. Charcoal smudges? That's part of the look. Ink bled? Now it's a texture. Every "mistake" is a discovery you can use intentionally later.

Mini-Project: 4x4 Style Studies

Draw the same simple subject (your hand, a coffee cup, or a crumpled paper bag) four times using four different techniques: stippling, scumbling, hatching, and expressive line work. Use at least two different mediums. Date the page and note which combination felt most "you."

Summary

  • Personal style comes from experimenting with tools and techniques
  • Stippling = dots, scumbling = circular scribbles, expressive lines = varied pressure
  • Try charcoal, ink, and watercolor pencil beyond just graphite
  • "Mistakes" with new mediums are discoveries
