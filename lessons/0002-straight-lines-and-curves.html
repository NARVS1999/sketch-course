Lesson 0002: Straight Lines & Curves

Parallel lines, cross-hatching, and S-curves — all without a ruler.

Phase 1: Foundation & Line Control

Lesson Goal Draw straight lines and smooth curves with consistent spacing and pressure — no ruler allowed.

Lines Are Everything

Every drawing is built from lines — straight ones, curved ones, long ones, short ones. The better you control your lines, the better everything else becomes. Your goal here is consistency, not speed.

Straight Lines

1

Draw two dots about 10 cm apart. Connect them with a single straight line in one swift motion (don't go slow — commit!).

2

Repeat 20 times. Aim for lines that hit both dots.

3

Now draw parallel lines: 10 vertical lines, equally spaced, all the same length. Then horizontal. Then diagonal.

Curves & S-Curves

1

Draw two dots. Connect them with a smooth C-curve (bowing up). Then a reverse C-curve (bowing down).

2

Draw an S-curve: start left, curve right, then curve back left to the endpoint. Make it smooth — no sharp corners.

3

Fill a whole page with parallel S-curves, like rolling hills. Keep the spacing even.

Tip Move from your shoulder, not your wrist, for long lines. Your shoulder gives you a bigger, smoother range of motion. Lock your wrist and pivot from the elbow or shoulder.

Quick Quiz

1. What body part should you move from for long, smooth lines?
2. What is the goal of parallel line practice?

Mini-Project: Line Grid

Draw a 10×10 grid of 5 cm squares entirely freehand. All lines should be straight, parallel, and evenly spaced. This is harder than it looks — and excellent training for your hand.

Summary

  • Draw straight lines with one swift motion — commit, don't hesitate
  • Move from your shoulder for long, smooth lines
  • Practice parallel lines and S-curves for control
  • Consistency matters more than speed
