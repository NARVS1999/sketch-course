Lesson 0011: Sight-Size Method

Measure your subject using a pencil — then transfer those proportions to paper.

Phase 3: Perspective & Proportion

Lesson Goal Use the sight-size method to measure and transfer a still life object's proportions accurately.

Proportions Made Practical

Proportions are the relationship between sizes: how many times the width of an object fits into its height, or how the size of one object relates to another nearby. The sight-size method gives you a simple, physical way to get proportions right.

How Sight-Size Works

1

Hold your pencil at arm's length, arm locked. Close one eye.

2

Align the pencil tip with the top of your subject. Slide your thumb down to where the bottom aligns. This is your "measurement."

3

Without moving your thumb, bring the pencil to your paper. Mark the top and bottom of your measurement.

4

Now measure the width the same way. Compare: how many widths fit in the height? This ratio is your proportion.

Example: Drawing a Mug

You're drawing a coffee mug on a table. Use sight-size to check:

  • Height vs. width: "The mug is 1.5× taller than it is wide"
  • The handle starts 1/3 down from the top
  • The opening is an ellipse with a width equal to 2/3 of the height

Check each relationship by measuring. Your eye will learn to see proportions over time.

Tip Check your measurements by flipping your pencil horizontally and vertically. This cross-checks that your proportions are accurate. Artists call this "plumb and level" — a vertical line (plumb) and horizontal line (level) to test alignment.

Mini-Project: Still Life Measurement

Set up 3 objects (a bottle, a cup, and a book). Before drawing, measure each one's height-to-width ratio using sight-size. Then draw them, checking each major proportion against your measurements. How close did you get?

Summary

  • Sight-size = pencil at arm's length to measure proportions
  • Measure height first, then width — compare the ratio
  • Check measurements by flipping vertical/horizontal
  • With practice, your eye will learn to see proportions naturally
