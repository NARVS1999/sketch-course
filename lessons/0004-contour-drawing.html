Lesson 0004: Contour Drawing

Draw the outline of an object without looking at your paper — pure observation.

Phase 1: Foundation & Line Control

Lesson Goal Train your eye to follow edges and your hand to follow along — building observation skills over symbol-drawing.

What Is Contour Drawing?

Contour drawing means drawing the edge of an object — where it meets the space around it. The most powerful version is blind contour: you look at the object the entire time and never look at your paper until you're done.

This feels weird at first, and your drawings will look funny. That's the point. Blind contour forces your brain to stop drawing "what it thinks it sees" (symbols) and start drawing what's actually there (edges).

Blind Contour Exercise

1

Place an object in front of you — a coffee mug, a shoe, a crumpled paper bag. Keep it simple.

2

Put your pencil on the paper. Look at the object. Pick a starting point on its edge.

3

Slowly trace the edge with your eyes. Your pencil should move at the exact same speed as your eyes. Do NOT look at your paper.

4

Follow every bump, dip, and curve. When you reach the starting point, stop. Now look at your paper.

Tip Don't peek. The magic of blind contour is that it breaks your brain's habit of drawing symbols (a "cookie-cutter" eye or nose) and forces you to draw what you actually see.

Variations

  • Modified contour — you may glance at your paper occasionally, but spend 90% of the time looking at the object
  • Cross-contour — draw lines that go across the form (like topographic lines on a map) to show volume
  • Your own hand — the classic exercise. Draw the contour of your non-drawing hand in a complex pose

Mini-Project: 5 Blind Contours

Draw 5 different objects using blind contour: your coffee mug, your phone, a plant leaf, your hand, and a crumpled piece of paper. Date each one. Don't judge them — they're not meant to look "good."

Summary

  • Contour drawing trains your eye-hand connection
  • Blind contour = don't look at the paper — it breaks the symbol-drawing habit
  • Follow edges slowly and carefully, matching eye speed to hand speed
  • Cross-contour adds lines across the form to show volume
