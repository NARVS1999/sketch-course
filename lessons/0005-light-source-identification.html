Lesson 0005: Light Source Identification

Learn where the light comes from — then map the highlights and shadows on any object.

Phase 2: Shading & Volume

Lesson Goal Identify the light source on any scene and draw a sphere with accurate highlight, mid-tone, core shadow, and cast shadow.

Light & Form

Without light, there is no form. Every object you draw exists in relation to a light source. Master this and your drawings will pop off the page.

The 5 Zones of Light on a Sphere

Draw a circle. Then identify these five zones:

  • Highlight — where light hits directly (leave it white)
  • Mid-tone — the transition zone between light and shadow
  • Terminator — the line where light stops hitting directly
  • Core Shadow — the darkest part on the object, just past the terminator
  • Cast Shadow — the shadow the object throws onto the ground or nearby surface
1

Draw a large circle on your page. This is your sphere.

2

Pick a light source direction (top-left is the default). Draw an arrow showing where light comes from.

3

Label where the highlight, mid-tone, terminator, core shadow, and cast shadow go.

4

Shade each zone with your pencil, leaving the highlight as white paper.

Tip Place a real object (like a ping-pong ball) under a desk lamp. Look for the five zones in real life before drawing them. This makes the concept concrete.

Quick Quiz

1. What is the darkest part of an object's own shadow called?
2. Where should you leave the paper white on a sphere?

Mini-Project: 3 Spheres, 3 Light Sources

Draw three spheres side by side. Shade each one with a different light source: top-left, top-right, and bottom (moonlight effect). Label all five zones on each.

Summary

  • Every form has 5 zones: highlight, mid-tone, terminator, core shadow, cast shadow
  • The core shadow is the darkest part on the object
  • The highlight is the brightest — save white paper for it
  • Always establish your light source before you start shading
